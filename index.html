About Me

I'm Alec, a 23-year-old physical design engineer from Chicago with a passion for building high-performance hardware systems. I’m currently a CPU Physical Design & Integration Intern at IBM and pursuing an M.S. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, where I also earned dual B.S. degrees in Electrical & Computer Engineering and Materials Science & Engineering.

That mindset naturally translated into a passion for physical implementation—where form meets function at the nanoscale. I'm driven by a desire to design efficient, next-generation ASIC chips that push the limits of performance, power, and scalability. I want to contribute to architectures that don't just meet current demands, but define what's possible for the future of computing

In my freetime, I am a major american football enthusiast and part-time rowing coach.
